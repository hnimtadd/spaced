Meaning of alcohol in English

alcohol
noun
us
/ˈæl.kə.hɑːl/ uk
/ˈæl.kə.hɒl/
Add to word list Add to word list
A2 [ U ]
drinks such as beer and wine that contain a substance that can make people drunk:
consumption of alcohol In Rhode Island, the legal age for purchase and consumption of alcohol is 21 years of age.
alcohol use Many of the young men sent here have a multitude of problems, including daily drug or alcohol use.
alcohol abuse
alcohol dependence
A2 [ U ]
a clear, liquid chemical substance, contained in alcoholic drinks, that is also used as a solvent (= a substance that dissolves another) and in fuel and medicines:
Most wines contain around twelve percent alcohol.
I could smell the alcohol on his breath.
Synonym
ethanol
See also
alcohol-free
low-alcohol
[ U or C ]   chemistry   specialized
an organic compound with one or more hydroxyl groups attached to a carbon atom of an alkyl group:
Methanol and ethanol are both alcohols, but methanol is acutely toxic.
More examplesFewer examples
  • He's under doctor's orders to cut down on fatty food and to drink no alcohol for at least six months.
  • No thanks, I never touch alcohol.
  • 28 percent of people asked thought that the advertising of tobacco and alcohol should be banned.
  • The situation was made more volatile by the fact that people had been drinking a lot of alcohol.
  • Experts say that we should not drink more than 14 units of alcohol a week.

alcohol | Intermediate English

alcohol
noun [ U ]
us
/ˈæl·kəˌhɔl/
Add to word list Add to word list
a liquid that is produced in making wine, beer, and liquor and that can cause changes in behavior in people who drink it:
Most wines contain about 12% alcohol.
chemistry
Alcohol is also any of a group of similar chemical substances used as solvents (= substances that dissolve others) and in fuel and medicines.
(Definition of alcohol from the Cambridge Academic Content Dictionary © Cambridge University Press)
